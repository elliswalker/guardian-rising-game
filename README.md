# Guardian Rising

**A Kingdom-style 2D strategy/defense game set in the Destiny universe.** You are a lone Guardian on a sparrow. You cannot hold the line yourself — you build the conditions, and your camp holds it for you.

> Ride. Recruit. Build. Survive the night. Take the fight to their door. Then do it on the next planet with nothing but what you carried.

*Current version: 0.0.1 (pre-beta). Solo dev project, built in the open.*

---

## The loop

Kingdom Two Crowns is the design baseline ([design philosophy](#design-rules)); Destiny 1 is the world. One verb, one resource, indirect power:

- **Glimmer is money AND armor.** Every hit you take sheds 10% of what you carry as scattered shards. Hit at zero glimmer, and they come for your Ghost. Carrying wealth is a risk decision; the vault (banker) keeps it safe but unarmored.
- **Frames are your subjects.** Toss glimmer at a dormant frame, then buy it a job at a post: **Redjacks** hunt by day and man the walls by night, **Sweeperbots** gather, **Builders** raise what you commission. You never command anyone directly.
- **The day is calm, the night is not.** Days run ~2 minutes. At dusk the enemy comes — from **both sides** on every planet after Earth. Spike waves land every fifth night; quiet recovery nights follow. Music crossfades day/night — the fade *is* the announcement.
- **Four planets, four sieges.** Earth (Fallen — the tutorial front), Cosmodrome (Fallen, dual-front), Moon (Hive — always night, surge floods, two tombs), Mars (Cabal — drop pods that land inside your yard, two launch sites). Kill each planet's bosses to break its portals.
- **Leaving is a decision.** Rebuild your crashed ship to leave. Planets decay while you're away unless you raised a **Beacon** (10/20/30 protected nights by tier). You keep glimmer, shards, and unlocks; you lose people and walls to the sim if you gambled.
- **Permadeath.** Lose your Ghost and the run is over. Autosave at dawn; death wipes it.

## Current feature set (v0.0.1)

- Full 4-planet campaign with away-simulation, save/load, victory and permadeath
- Glimmer-as-armor economy: shed-on-hit, pour-to-bribe, worker kits, vault deposit/withdraw
- Encampment ladder T1–T4 (builders+defenders → sweepers+towers → vault → the Charge)
- Per-planet camps and ships — every world is rebuilt from scratch on arrival
- 3 supers (Golden Gun, Striker Smash, Well of Radiance) via Ghost shrines; legendary shards + motes
- 2 hermits (Gunsmith → Ballista tower, Tinker → Flare tower), material tiers gated by Foundries
- Charge banners that plant at your outermost wall, one per front
- Generated pixel art under a written art bible (silhouette-first, one sky light, K2C ramps), 2-frame walk cycles for all 9 ground enemies, parallax with true foreground layers, Earth running on hand-made background plates
- Generated ambient music (day pad / night drone) with slow crossfades on real audio buses
- Main menu + pause menu (Kingdom rules: the world is the menu), guardian palette swap on new runs, persistent audio/video options

## Controls

| Input | Action |
|---|---|
| `A` / `D` or `←` / `→` | Ride left / right (double-tap to sprint) |
| `Space` | The one verb — build, recruit, pay, board (hold to pour glimmer) |
| `E` | Super / Ghost ability (once attuned at a shrine) |
| `↑` / `↓` | Menu navigation; planet select on the ship |
| `Esc` | Pause — resume, options, save & quit |
| `F1` | Debug panel |

## Running from source

1. Install [Godot 4.7](https://godotengine.org/) (GL Compatibility renderer is used).
2. Clone, open `project.godot`, press F5. First import takes a moment (all art is PNG-generated).

No third-party assets, addons, or plugins — every sprite, background, and sound in the repo is generated by the Python tools in `tools/` (Python 3 + Pillow, stdlib `wave` for audio). Regenerate any set by running its `tools/gen_*.py` from the repo root.

## Design rules

The project follows a written art & design bible (kept with the design docs, private vault):

1. No text in the world — teaching is consequence; prompts are scaffolding until the art carries it
2. One verb (Space), one currency (glimmer), every resource dual-purpose
3. Protect the calm — daytime is genuinely peaceful; the tension is priced in serenity
4. Diegetic audio over HUD — the crossfade is the announcement, the beacon lamp is the status readout
5. Silhouette-first pixel art, one sky light, saturation reserved for meaning
